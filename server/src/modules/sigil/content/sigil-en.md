# 1. State Background

The State Background shows **which federal state the city belongs to**.

## Procedure

1. The starting point is the **flag of the federal state**.
2. The flag is **simplified**:
   - Coats of arms are removed.
   - Complex patterns are reduced.
3. The colors of the flag are transferred as **horizontal stripes** into a circle, except for Hamburg.

## Example

Baden-Württemberg → black on top, yellow on bottom
Lower Saxony → black, red, yellow
Schleswig-Holstein → blue, white, red

# 2. Population Frame

The Population Frame shows the **size of the city** based on its population.

| Population | Class | Frame Type |
|-----|-----|-----|
| up to 500,000 | Medium City | uniform green border |
| 500,001 – 1,000,000 | Large City | four markers on the border (top, bottom, left, right) |
| over 1,000,000 | Megacity | four markers + significantly wider green border

## Example

Münster (316,403 inhabitants) → Medium City
Dortmund (587,696 inhabitants) → Large City
Berlin (3,664,088 inhabitants) → Megacity

# 3. Capital Crown

Some cities receive a **Capital Crown at the top inside the sigil**.

The Capital Crown indicates the **political status of the city**.

| Status | Appearance |
|-----|-----|
| Federal Capital | golden line crown, below it a red and a golden bar |
| State Capital | golden line crown, below it a red bar |
| Former Federal Capital Bonn | golden line crown |
| No capital status | no Capital Crown |

## Example

Berlin → Federal Capital Crown
Munich → State Capital Crown
Bonn → Special Case Crown
Cologne → no crown

# 4. Orientation Disk (Location within the Federal State)

In the lower area of the sigil sits the **Orientation Disk**.

It contains a **Location Disk** — a golden dot that shows **where the city is located within its federal state**.

## Cardinal Directions

| Location | Position of the Location Disk |
|-----|-----|
| North | top |
| South | bottom |
| East | right |
| West | left |
| Northeast | top right |
| Northwest | top left |
| Southeast | bottom right |
| Southwest | bottom left |
| Center | centered |

## City-States

For city-states (Berlin, Hamburg, Bremen) a special rule applies:

- the Location Disk is **larger**
- it is placed **centrally**

This indicates that city and federal state are **identical**.

## Example

Munich (Location: South) → Location Disk at the bottom
Dresden (Location: East) → Location Disk on the right
Hamburg (City-State) → large Location Disk centered

<!-- SECTION 5 -->
# 5. Founding Center

At the center of the sigil sits the **Founding Center** — a **black circle with a number**.

This number indicates the **century the city was founded in**.

The founding year is **rounded up to the next century**.

## Example

| Founding Year | Display |
|-----|-----|
| 1158 | 12 |
| 1299 | 13 |
| 700 | 7 |

The number is displayed **in golden text inside the Founding Center**.

<!-- SECTION 6 -->
# 6. Short Registration Plate

On the left side of the sigil, the **Short Registration Plate** (vehicle registration code of the city) is displayed.

## Appearance

- rectangular field
- on the left a **blue stripe**
- on the right a **white field**
- the Short Registration Plate in black text inside

## Example

Munich → **M**

<!-- SECTION 7 -->
# 7. Coordinate Rectangle

On the right side of the sigil sits the **Coordinate Rectangle** with geographic coordinates.

## Appearance

- white rectangle
- light blue border
- Format: **Latitude|Longitude** (truncated to integers, separated by a vertical bar)

## Example

Munich (48.1371° N, 11.5755° E) → **48|11**
